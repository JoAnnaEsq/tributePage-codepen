Elizabeth I of England

Major Events in Elizabeth's Life

  • September 7, 1533: Elizabeth Tudor is born at Greenwich Palace.
  • November 17, 1558: Princess Elizabeth becomes Queen of England upon the death of her sister, Queen Mary.
  • January 15, 1559: Elizabeth's coronation is held at Westminster Abbey.
  • Between 1584 - 1589: Sir Walter Raleigh helps start a colony on the eastern coast of the "New World" and names it Virginia after his "Virgin Queen."
  • February 1, 1587: Elizabeth signs the death warrant for the execution of her cousin, Queen Mary of Scotland, after Mary is convicted of treason for her involvement in numerous plots to depose Elizabeth.
  • May 1588: King Philip of Spain launches the Spanish Armada to invade England.
  • August 19, 1588: At Tilbury, Elizabeth addresses her troops before the Royal Fleet's engages with the Armada in a decisive battle and forces them out of the English Channel.
  • March 24, 1603: Queen Elizabeth I dies at Richmond Palace, and the Tudor Dynasty ends.
  • April 28, 1603: Elizabeth is buried at Westminster Abbey.

Famous Quotes

On Fitness to Lead:

I know I have the body of a weak and feeble woman, but I have the heart and stomach of a King, and of a King of England too.

— Elizabeth I

On Marriage:

I would rather be a beggar and single than a queen and married.

— Elizabeth I

On Royalty:

To be a king and wear a crown is a thing more glorious to them that see it than it is pleasant to them that bear it.

— Elizabeth I
